Elon Musk

Background

Who is Elon Musk?

Elon Musk is an American entrepreneur owning various companies such as, SpaceX, PayPal, Tesla, OpenAI, and Neuralink and is the world’s richest person with a net worth of $393 billion, as of 2025. He was born June 28th, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa to a South African father and a Canadian Mother, displaying an early talent for computers and entrepreneurship. He created a video game called Blastar at 12 years old and sold it to a computer magazine for $500, and left high school, wanting to avoid mandatory military service in South Africa, at 17 years old, moving to Canada in 1988.

Path To Fame

Musk studied business for 2 years at Queen’s University before relocating to the United States to pursue business and physics at the University of Pennsylvania, where he received bachelor's degrees in both subjects.

Soon after, Musk enrolled at Stanford to pursue his doctorate in physics, but left after only participating for a few days, to cofound Zip2, an online content publishing company that helps businesses establish an online presence.

Zip2 was bought off by Compaq Computer Corporation for about $307 million in 1999, where Musk acquired around $22 million for his stake in the company.

Following this sale, Musk co-founded X.com, an online payment company that later merged to form PayPal, which was acquired by eBay in 2002 for $1.5 Billion.

That same year, Musk founded the space technology company SpaceX, becoming its CEO and chief engineer.

In 2004, he became one of the major funders of Tesla Motors (later renamed Tesla), an electric car company founded by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning. He later became CEO and in 2006, introduced Tesla’s first car, the Roadster, which could travel 394 km on a single charge.

OpenAI was then co-founded by Musk in December 2015, however, three years later Musk left the artificial intelligence research company due to conflicts about its direction.

Neuralink was Elon’s next big company, founded in 2016, it aims to develop implantable brain-machine interfaces that can communicate with phones and computers as well as help people with paralysis.

The Boring Company was founded that same year, an American construction company whose projects are primarily designed for intra-city transit systems to solve traffic challenges and provide transportation.

In 2022, Twitter was acquired by Musk for $44 billion who rebranded it as “X” to create an “everything app” similar to WeChat in China, which provides messaging, social media, payments, and services in one platform.

Musk announces the launch of a new artificial intelligence company, xAI, in July, 2023, created to compete with OpenAI’s ChatGPT.

Impact

Space Exploration

In space, Musk is an important figure to the United States’ ambitions, his company SpaceX dominates commercial spaceflight and has made it much more accessible.

Transportation

Musk’s impact on the automotive industry cannot be overstated. He has popularized electric vehicles, advanced battery technology, and pushed autonomous driving capabilities.

Neurotechnology

Elon pioneered commercial brain-computer interface technologies through Neuralink, providing a future solution to people with paralysis and neurological conditions.
